Real life example elections using IRV

Burlington VT 2009 mayorial election[1]: Presents an example in non-monotonicity. Click here for the real totals and here for the hypothetical totals. In this example the candidates are: Bob Kiss (A), Andy Montroll (B), and Kurt Wright (C). If all 495 of the C>A>B voters and 258 of the C-only voters had instead voted for A, then C would have been eliminated in the first round, then A would have lost to B. So? This means that by increasing their support for Mr. Kiss (A), those voters caused him to lose.
